FAQ

Where does this data come from?

The Nebraska Accountability and Disclosure Commission publishes data every week that reports donations, loans, spending and other campaign activity. World-Herald reporters programmed a method to download, parse and display those transactions.

How is this different from NADC's own website?

Most importantly, our site lets you search by donor name. For the first time, you can see how much a person or group has donated, and see where their money went: Citizens can start drawing connections between the people and organizations financing state and local elections. We also aggregate information at the transaction level. Campaigns report only donations over $250, though smaller donations are reflected in summary totals reported to the NADC. Because we use only reportable transactions, our totals are different from what the NADC reports.

Where are contributions to president, senate and other federal races?

Federal contributions are not included here. To explore that data, we recommend the Center for Responsive Politics' search tool.

Why isn't there more information about committees?

We can report named officers for a committee and the city where it is based. Details on a committee's purpose are not reported to the NADC, but that information can usually be gleaned by looking at a committee's spending, donations, donors and the like.

Why do some donors show up multiple times?

The NADC makes no attempt to standardize donors. Gov. Peter Ricketts, for example, appears in the database as Peter Ricketts, Pete Ricketts and J. Peter Ricketts. To the NADC, these are all different people. For large donors such as Gov. Ricketts, the World-Herald has attempted to standardize these records. Many donors still appear multiple times, however.

If you see records that ought to be standardized, please contact us.

Models

• Entity: Any group, committee, donor, lender or other entity that has been assigned a unique ID by the NADC. This is the parent table.

• Donation: Money, in-kind donations, "direct expenditures" to a committee, pledges.

• Candidate: Candidates tied to campaign committees and ballot questions.

• Loans

• Expenditure: Includes both administrative expenses and "targeted" expenses that were earmarked to support or oppose a candidate or committee.
